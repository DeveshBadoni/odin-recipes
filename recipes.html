The Indian Cuisine

Breakfast

1. Aaloo Parantha

Aaloo parantha is an indian breakfast consisting of 'Roti' filled with potato stuffing consisting of various spices and chilis, which is served either with butter or dahi or achar.

2. Poha

Poha is an indian cuisine made of rice which is cooked with minimal spices and peanuts and chillies and sometimes Namkeen is added to it enhance its taste. A gist of lemon takes the taste to a new level.

Lunch

Raajma Chawal

Rajma Chawal is a very famous indian cuisine that is made from Red Kidney Beans pulses and Rice. It is rich in taste and always creates a joyous atmosphere whenever cooked in any household.

Snacks

Chai

The indian cuisine is never completed without a cup of tea. Well, we do drink a lot of tea and many times a day, but lets just say it's a very popular snack time drink.

Dinner

Roti and Mix Veg

Indians usually eat roti and vegetables at night. The Mix Veg is a mixture of different vegetables and tastes superb.

Sweets

No indian dish is complete without sweets.

Rasmalai

Rasmalai is a sweet that just melts in your mouth and the sweet flavour just fills you with joy.

Gajar Halwa

Gajar Halwa or Carrot Halwa is a sweet dish made using carrots, sugar, condensed milk, mawa and dry fruits, which are cooked in Desi Ghee to provide it with its rich flavour.
